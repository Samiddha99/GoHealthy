Why This Website?

We all know what's the situation of healthcare system in India. Healthcare system facing shortage of beds, medical oxygen, hospitals, doctors. But since a long decades the State never response to fix the problems. And result of that is painful death. A strong and effective action should be taken to make a better healthcare system.
Go Healthy is a website for medical support, we want to help the victims of that poor healthcare system. Purpose of the website is to deliver all details about Bed and Antivenom availability in all hospitals, Doctors and Blood Donor near you and how to do first aid. This website has four different features. In Check Hospital section user can see the all information about all the Hospital and number of available bed in Hospital and also can book bed for patients and can see which hospital has Antivenom. In Find Doctor section user can see all the information about doctors of their location. And in Find Donor section user can see the all information about blood donors. In First Aid section there are several free videos and photos are available which will provide you a guide about First Aid.

Reality of The Healthcare System

India ranked 145 out of 195 countries in Healthcare Access and Quality Index. Since Independent no serious and effective action has been taken by the Government to improve the healthcare system. And due to the government negligence a huge number of people are depriving from the healthcare service and many patients are die due to poor healthcare system. We believe that it is everybody's right to get the healthcare services easily. Every month many patients losing their lives because they don't get bed in hospital, don't get Blood, don't get medical support; and many snake bitten patients are die due to no Antivenom in hospitals.

Data Says that -
  • Bed availability of India is only 5 beds for 10,000 people. In Human Development Report 2020, India ranked 155 out of 167 countries on Bed Availability. Due to the lack of beds in hospitals people are struggling to admit the patients.
  • India has only 69,265 Hospitals. Just 25,778 Government Hospitals and 43,487 Private Hospitals. Due to the low number of people hospitals, rural people are unable to admit patient within a short time because they have go to city to admit the patients in a hospital.
  • According to Human Development Index 2020, India has just 8.6 doctors for every 10,000 population (i.e. 1 doctor for every 1163 people). But WHO recommended a country must have at least 1 doctor for every 1000 people. So that there have huge work pressure on each doctors to treat the large number of patients. This small number of doctors are not able to take care all the patients.
  • 27% death is for lack of Medical Attention - Data 2016.
  • According to Harvard study (2018), 5.2 million death occur in India annually due to medical errors.
  • Only 600 (20%) species of snakes are venomous out of over 3000 species of snakes in the world. And in India only 60 species of snakes (i.e. 22%) are venomous out of 270 snake species, but almost 45,900 snakebite deaths occur each year in India; because shortage of anti-venom in healthcare centers increases the possibility of death of snake bite patient.
  • In every 2 seconds, someone needs blood. India facing blood shortage of almost 12 lakh unit. And India has more than 512 million eligible blood donors. This number is enough to fulfill the shortage. WHO recommends that 1 percent of a country’s population would suffice for its blood needs. Annually 10 million blood are being collected. But many blood are being discarded before the expire date due to poor blood storage infrastructure. Poor blood storage infrastructure reduces the capacity of the blood banks.
  • In a year 4,37,396 road accident were recorded and 1,54,732 death were recorded because of road accident - NCRB data 2019. In some cases, cause of death in road accident is, delay in medical treatment.
  • Public health expenditure is only 3% of country's GDP, it's needs to be increased at least up to 7%.

If a patient get an ambulance any way, then the patient have to do struggle to get a bed in hospital, if the patient get a bed, then again the patient have to do struggle to get proper medical support in the hospital.

It is the consequence of negligence of problems in healthcare system by the Government since a long decades.

Problems in the Healthcare System We Focused

There are lots of problems in the healthcare system. We focus on some of the problems. The problems that we focused are-

  • Patients died due to not being admitted to the Hospital within right time:

    • If you read news each day, you find such news that the patient died due to not getting bed in hospital. In the COVID-19 pandemic, this become more deadly. Shortage of bed in hospitals, is one of the main reason of highest no. of death of COVID-19 patients. Patient parties keep running from one hospital to another hospital to admit the patient - it consumes so many valuable times, but hospitals replies we have no more beds, and so that the patient dies. If the patient didn’t admit to the hospital, then how he/she will receive treatment? We also see that in many hospitals due to long admission process, it wastes the times - and result is death.

  • Patients died due to not getting Oxygen:

    • Some hospitals have not oxygen beds, and also some time hospitals have no oxygen; as a result of this patient’s families have to suffer to find a hospital with oxygen availability to admit the patient. The COVID-19 pandemic exposed the shortage of oxygen. A huge number of COVID-19 patients died just for shortage of oxygen in hospitals.

  • Snake Bite Patients died due to not get Antivenom at right time:

    • 45,900 snakebite deaths occur each year in India, but in india most of the snake is non-poisonous; only 60 species of snakes are venomous out of 270 snake species. But still why number of death is high? Because in most of the hospitals, specially in ruler hospitals, there have no antivenom. But most of snake bit incident happening in ruler area. So many valuable time is waste to find a hospital which hospital has Antivenom to give treatment to a sake bite patient. Hence, shortage of anti-venom put thousands of lives at risk.

  • People do not able to contact doctor in emergency in many cases:

    • India doesn’t have sufficient doctors. As a result many people can’t contact to the doctors at the right time. Those who live far away from the hospital or in ruler area, for them, it is not possible to get medical support quickly due to lack of rural transport infrastructure. In that case they need to contact/ consult with a doctor to get medical support. But if they don’t have any such platform how do they do it?

  • Patients died due to not receive Blood within right time:

    • In every 2 seconds, someone needs blood. Every year many patients are losing theirs life just for not being able to collect blood. In India Blood Banks have not sufficient bloods, for that reasons, patient's family need to collect blood from donors. But it is impossible for them to contact a blood donor. Is the patient’s family go to everybody in their area and ask their blood group and request them to donate blood? If patient’s family do that, it will waste the time; and waste of time means the patient is certain to die.

  • The accident victims die due to not getting medical help at the right time:

Due to shortage MST, Traffic Jump, lack of Medical Infrastructure Medical Support Team do late in their works.
